## Common Immigration Questions

### How long does the green card process take?
Processing times vary significantly depending on the category of green card and current USCIS backlogs. Family-based green cards can take anywhere from 1-5+ years depending on the relationship and country of origin. Employment-based green cards also vary widely. We recommend checking the USCIS processing times for the most current estimates.

### What documents do I need to bring to my consultation?
For your initial consultation, please bring:
- Valid passport and any current immigration documents
- Any notices or correspondence from USCIS
- Employment records (if applicable)
- Family documents (birth certificates, marriage certificates)
- Any previous immigration applications or petitions

### Can I work while my application is pending?
This depends on your current immigration status and the type of application filed. Some applicants may be eligible for Employment Authorization Documents (EAD). We can advise you on your specific situation during your consultation.

### What happens if my visa expires while my application is pending?
If you have a pending application with USCIS, you may be in a period of "authorized stay" depending on the type of application. However, this is a complex area of immigration law and each case is different. Please consult with us to understand your specific situation.

## Emergency Situations

### What should I do if I'm detained by ICE?
If you or a family member is detained, contact our office immediately. Do not sign any documents without consulting an attorney. You have the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

### I received a Notice to Appear. What should I do?
A Notice to Appear (NTA) is a serious document that begins removal proceedings. Contact our office immediately to discuss your options. Do not ignore this notice.
